# COMMODORE INFORMATION NETWORK (CompuServe paperless magazine)

**Summary:** Description of the Commodore Information Network available via CompuServe, including connection instructions using a modem and VICMODEM, the access command "G CBM", menu department names (Main Menu, Dealers, User Questions, Magazines, etc.), and service/contact details.

**Overview**
The Commodore Information Network is a "paperless magazine" hosted on the CompuServe online service and accessible to Commodore users via telephone modem. It supplements POWER/PLAY and COMMODORE magazines with up-to-the-minute product news, software announcements, user-group information, technical tips, and other Commodore-specific departments. Commodore included a free one-year CompuServe subscription with each VICMODEM package.

Key published details preserved from the source:
- Planned future service (early 1983): downloadable program listings directly from the Information Network.
- Intended audience: Commodore owners seeking product announcements, user help, dealer info, magazines/newsletters, and technical/programming tips.
- Contact for CompuServe customer service: 800-848-8990 (in Ohio: 614-457-8600).

**Connecting with a Modem and Accessing the Network**
- **Required hardware/software:** A Commodore computer, a telephone-line modem (e.g., VICMODEM), and a CompuServe subscription.
- **Connection steps:**
  1. **Set up the modem:**
     - Ensure the modem is in "originate" mode. For VICMODEM, this is typically set to "O" (originate).
  2. **Dial the access number:**
     - Use the modem to dial your local CompuServe access number.
  3. **Log in to CompuServe:**
     - Upon connection, the CompuServe system will prompt for your User ID and password.

**Menu Departments (Table of Contents)**
The Commodore Information Network offers sixteen departments accessible from its main menu:

- **Main Menu Description:** Overview of the network's structure and available services.
- **Direct Access Codes:** Shortcuts to navigate directly to specific sections or services within the network.
- **Special Commands:** Commands that provide additional functionalities or shortcuts within the network.
- **User Questions:** Section where users can post questions and receive answers from the community or experts.
- **Public Bulletin Board:** A forum for users to post messages, announcements, or discussions accessible to all members.
- **Magazines and Newsletters:** Access to digital versions of Commodore-related publications and newsletters.
- **Products Announced:** Information on newly released or upcoming Commodore products.
- **Commodore News Direct:** Official news and press releases from Commodore.
- **Commodore Dealers:** Directory and information about authorized Commodore dealers.
- **Educational Resources:** Materials and information aimed at educational applications of Commodore computers.
- **User Groups:** Information about local and national Commodore user groups.
- **Descriptions:** Detailed descriptions of various Commodore products and services.
- **Questions and Answers:** A curated section of frequently asked questions and their answers.
- **Software Tips:** Tips and tricks related to Commodore software usage and development.
- **Technical Tips:** Technical advice and insights for advanced users and developers.
- **Directory Descriptions:** Descriptions and navigation aids for the network's directory structure.

These department names are taken verbatim from the menu table in the source.
