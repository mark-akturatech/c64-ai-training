# Nibble mapping — Hex E (0xE)

**Summary:** 4-bit nibble mapping for hexadecimal E ($E): binary pattern 1110 (bits b3..b0 = 1 1 1 0), bit weights 8+4+2+0 = decimal 14. Searchable terms: nibble, 4-bit, binary 1110, decimal 14, hex $E.

**Description**
A nibble is a 4-bit unit consisting of bits b3, b2, b1, and b0, with respective weights of 8, 4, 2, and 1. The hexadecimal digit E (0xE) is represented by the binary pattern 1110, where:

- **b3 = 1** → contributes 8
- **b2 = 1** → contributes 4
- **b1 = 1** → contributes 2
- **b0 = 0** → contributes 0

Summing these contributions: 8 + 4 + 2 + 0 = 14 decimal, which corresponds to 0xE in hexadecimal.

Canonical representations:
- **Binary:** 1110
- **Decimal:** 14
- **Hexadecimal:** $E (0xE)

Bit-position notation (most significant bit on the left):
- **b3 b2 b1 b0 = 1 1 1 0**
