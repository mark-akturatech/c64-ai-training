**Summary:** Guidance for C-64 demo programmers to study demos and external references; focus on inspecting techniques such as chars vs sprites, scrolling, and realtime graphics updates. Encourages consulting magazines, articles, and an expanded list under "references_lines".

**Study Recommendations**

- **Learn by Inspection:** When watching a demo, identify which effects are implemented with character graphics (chars) and which use hardware sprites; note where smooth or hardware-assisted scrolling is used versus where the program updates bitmap/character data in real-time.
- **Focus on Techniques:** Dissecting techniques (chars, sprites, scrolling, real-time graphics updates) rather than just the visible effect yields reusable implementation ideas.
- **Consult External Documents:** Refer to magazines and articles for deeper technical coverage and implementation examples; many demo techniques were documented in periodicals and notes by demo authors.
- **Explore Demo Archives:** Study specific demos to understand the techniques they demonstrate.

**Recommended Documents and Magazine Articles**

- **Ahoy! Magazine:** Published from 1984 to 1989, covering Commodore computers with articles on programming and graphics techniques. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahoy%21?utm_source=openai))
- **Compute!'s Gazette:** A monthly magazine from 1983 to 1995, featuring type-in programs, tutorials, and technical articles for the Commodore 64. ([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compute%21%27s_Gazette?utm_source=openai))
- **The Transactor:** Known for in-depth technical articles on Commodore hardware and software, providing valuable insights for programmers. ([c64programming.wordpress.com](https://c64programming.wordpress.com/2013/06/07/best-c64-magazine-for-programmers/?utm_source=openai))
- **RUN Magazine:** Focused on Commodore 8-bit computers, offering programming tutorials and software reviews. ([commodore.ca](https://www.commodore.ca/commodore-gallery/run-magazine/?utm_source=openai))

**Demo Archives and Downloads**

- **C64.CH - The C64 Demo Portal:** A comprehensive archive of C64 demos, including downloads and information on various productions. ([c64.ch](https://c64.ch/?utm_source=openai))
- **CSDb (Commodore 64 Scene Database):** An extensive database of C64 demos, groups, and sceners, offering downloads and detailed information. ([diskmags.de](https://www.diskmags.de/index.php/CSDB?utm_source=openai))
- **C64 XLFiles:** Hosts large collections of C64 games, demos, music, and magazines for download. ([c64.rulez.org](https://c64.rulez.org/xlfiles/?utm_source=openai))

**Specific Demos to Study and Techniques Demonstrated**

- **"Edge of Disgrace" by Booze Design:** Demonstrates advanced sprite multiplexing, smooth scrolling, and real-time graphics updates.
- **"Deus Ex Machina" by Crest:** Showcases complex character graphics manipulation and innovative use of hardware sprites.
- **"Dutch Breeze" by Blackmail:** Features extensive use of hardware-assisted scrolling and real-time bitmap updates.
- **"Second Reality" by Smash Designs:** An adaptation of the PC demo, illustrating cross-platform techniques and effects.
